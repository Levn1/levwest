Lev West Limited

Thoughtful eCommerce, brand and digital work from the UK.

Lev West is a small studio operating out of the United Kingdom, working across eCommerce, digital marketing, brand development and copywriting. We collaborate with selected partners and build projects of our own, staying close to the details while keeping a long-term view of the brands we touch.

Our work often sits at the intersection of strategy and execution: designing how a brand should feel, then carrying that through into products, platforms and day-to-day communication with customers.

A Quiet Portfolio

Alongside client work, Lev West develops its own online projects and brands, experimenting with products, customer journeys and operating models in real time. Insights from those experiments inform how we work with others.

What we do

Commerce, content and everything around them.

Lev West Limited works with businesses that sell primarily online: direct-to-consumer brands, marketplace operators, and product companies looking to refine or extend their digital presence. Engagements can be narrow and tactical or broader and ongoing, depending on what is needed.

Typical work includes shaping brand narratives, establishing tone of voice, developing product stories, and aligning on how a business is presented across channels — from website copy and landing pages through to emails, ads and packaging.

We also help existing eCommerce operations review their foundations: product architecture, on-site flows, content hierarchy and the small details that tend to influence how customers move, read and decide.

How we operate

Deliberate, collaborative, and deliberately small.

Lev West is intentionally lean. Most work is handled directly by the core team, with specialists brought in where useful for design, development or channel-specific execution. This keeps communication simple and ensures projects stay close to their original intent.

We work with a limited number of clients at any given time and usually on a project-by-project or retained basis. Some relationships are centred around a single launch or campaign; others involve ongoing support as brands grow, test and adapt.

The common thread is clarity: making sure that brands know what they are trying to say, how they want to sound, and what role each piece of communication plays in the broader commercial picture.
